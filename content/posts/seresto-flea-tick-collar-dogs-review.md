## How the Seresto Collar Works

Unlike traditional flea collars that simply repel insects with a strong-smelling chemical concentrated near the collar, the Seresto uses a patented **slow-release polymer matrix technology**. Two active ingredients — **imidacloprid** (10%) and **flumethrin** (4.5%) — are embedded throughout the collar material and released in low concentrations continuously over 8 months.

The active ingredients spread from the collar across your dog's skin and coat through the natural oils in the fur (translocation), reaching the entire body surface within 24 hours of first application. This means protection isn't just concentrated around the neck — it covers your dog from nose to tail.

**Imidacloprid** targets the nervous system of fleas and flea larvae, killing fleas through contact before they can bite. **Flumethrin** is a pyrethroid that repels and kills ticks, including deer ticks (which carry Lyme disease), American dog ticks, brown dog ticks, and lone star ticks.

The result is a dual-action system that both **kills on contact** (fleas die without needing to bite) and **repels** (ticks are deterred from attaching in the first place).

## Flea Protection: Kills Before the Bite

One of the most important distinctions in flea prevention is **whether the treatment requires a bite to work**. Many oral flea treatments (like NexGard or Bravecto) require a flea to bite your dog before the active ingredient kills it. This means your dog still experiences flea bites during the process.

The Seresto collar kills fleas **through contact** — fleas that land on your dog's coat are exposed to the active ingredients without needing to reach the skin and bite. This is particularly important for dogs with flea allergy dermatitis (FAD), where even a single flea bite can trigger a significant allergic reaction. For FAD-prone dogs, a contact-kill product is meaningfully better than a bite-activated one.

Flea eggs and larvae in the environment are also affected when your dog moves through areas where they're present — the active ingredients on your dog's coat help break the flea life cycle in your home over time.

## Tick Coverage: Which Ticks Does It Protect Against?

<img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/61YGRWPMpzL._AC_SL1500_.jpg" alt="Seresto collar tick protection coverage deer tick American dog tick" style="width:100%;max-width:700px;display:block;margin:24px auto;border-radius:12px" />

The Seresto collar for dogs over 18 lbs is labeled for protection against four tick species:

- **Deer ticks (black-legged ticks)** — primary vector for Lyme disease, anaplasmosis, and babesiosis
- **American dog ticks** — vector for Rocky Mountain spotted fever and tularemia
- **Brown dog ticks** — can infest homes and transmit Ehrlichia and Rocky Mountain spotted fever
- **Lone star ticks** — vector for ehrlichiosis, tularemia, and STARI

For dogs in wooded, grassy, or brushy environments — or in regions where Lyme disease is endemic — this tick coverage is comprehensive. The collar repels ticks before attachment, which is critical because Lyme disease transmission from a deer tick typically requires 36–48 hours of attachment. Repelling ticks before they attach eliminates that transmission window entirely.

## Safety Features: The Breakaway Mechanism

A legitimate concern with any dog collar is the risk of snagging — if a dog's collar catches on a fence, branch, or crate wire, a non-breakaway collar can cause serious injury. The Seresto collar includes a **safety release mechanism** built into the clasp: under sufficient sustained pressure (such as a dog pulling against a snagged collar), the clasp releases, preventing strangulation.

This breakaway feature does not affect the collar during normal wear. The clasp holds securely under typical movement, play, and activity. Only sustained directional force — the kind that occurs during a snag — triggers the release.

For dogs that wear a separate identification or leash collar, the Seresto is designed to be worn in addition to the regular collar, not as a replacement. It can be worn underneath a walking collar without interference.

The Seresto collar has an essentially **odorless profile** from the perspective of human detection.

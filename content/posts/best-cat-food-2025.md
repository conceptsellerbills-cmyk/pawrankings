# Best Cat Food 2025: Top 9 Brands for Healthy, Happy Cats

Cats are obligate carnivores — they require specific nutrients (taurine, arachidonic acid, vitamin A) that can only come from animal tissue. This makes cat food selection more biologically consequential than most pet owners realize.

The right food supports heart health, urinary health, healthy weight, and long life. Here are the 9 best cat food brands for 2025.

## What Cats Actually Need

**High protein:** Cats use protein (not carbohydrates) as their primary energy source. Look for 30-40%+ protein in dry food, 40-55%+ in wet food.

**Taurine:** Essential amino acid cats can't synthesize — must come from food. Deficiency causes heart disease (dilated cardiomyopathy) and blindness. Any AAFCO-complete food contains adequate taurine.

**Low carbohydrates:** Cats have limited ability to digest starches. High-carb dry foods can contribute to obesity and diabetes in cats.

**Moisture:** Cats evolved from desert animals with low thirst drive. They're chronically under-hydrated on dry-only diets, which contributes to urinary and kidney issues. Wet food provides critical moisture.

## Wet Food vs Dry Food for Cats

**The veterinary consensus in 2025:** Wet food is strongly preferred, especially for:
- Male cats (prone to urinary crystals)
- Cats with kidney disease (70%+ of cats over 15 have some kidney impairment)
- Overweight cats (lower calorie density with high satiety)
- Cats who don't drink enough water

If you feed dry food, a water fountain significantly increases water intake.

**Ideal approach:** Wet food as primary diet, with dry available for grazing if desired. Or 50/50 wet/dry.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is grain-free cat food better?
Unlike with dogs, there's no established link between grain-free cat food and heart disease. Cats naturally eat low-carb diets. However, grain-free doesn't automatically mean better — check protein content and ingredient quality.

### How often should cats eat?
Twice daily feeding is most common and appropriate for adult cats. Free-feeding (dry food always available) contributes to obesity in many cats.

### My cat is very picky — what helps?
Try warm wet food (slightly microwave canned food). Try different textures (pâté vs. chunks). Mix a small amount of new food with current food and gradually change the ratio. Some cats prefer specific proteins — try novel proteins like rabbit or venison.
